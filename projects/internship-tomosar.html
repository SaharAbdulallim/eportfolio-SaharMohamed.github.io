Internship — Deep Learning for TomoSAR

Host: EO Analytics, Salzburg University, Austria • Supervisor: Dr. Karima Hadj-Rabah

Internship Report: Forest Canopy and Ground Height Estimation using TomoSAR and Deep Learning Models

Intern: Sahar Mohamed
Period: July 2025 – September 2025

Lab & Context

Lab: EO Analytics, Salzburg University, Austria. The project used TomoSAR data (TropiSAR) for forest height estimation in Paracou, French Guiana. The goal was to estimate canopy and ground heights using deep learning and compare with traditional spectral/Tomographic methods.

Tasks

  1. Training a Tomographic SAR Neural Network (TSNN)
    Trained a TSNN model to estimate forest canopy and ground heights in the Paracou region. Dataset split into training/validation/test; models tested on unlabeled data to evaluate generalization; training tweaks applied to improve performance.
  2. Literature Review and Performance Comparison
    Reviewed recent articles and compared TSNN performance with state-of-the-art approaches from the literature.
  3. Development of Modified UNet Model (M-UNET)
    Developed a modified UNet architecture (M-UNET) to estimate canopy and ground heights from patches (to capture spatial context). Evaluated on unlabeled datasets to assess generalization where lidar was not available.
  4. Comparison with Traditional Methods
    Compared TSNN and M-UNET with spectral estimation techniques (CAPON and MUSIC) to evaluate relative performance.

Learning Objectives

The internship aimed to deepen understanding of forest height estimation from TomoSAR and to build DL models that estimate canopy/top-of-canopy heights without reliance on lidar. The practical experience included handling TomoSAR covariance matrices from fully-polarized SLC images (HH, HV, VV), model training, and result interpretation. This complemented prior BIP drone work where lidar was costly and limited in spatial coverage.

Recommendations for Future Intern

  • Understand SAR fundamentals: polarizations, processing chains, and data formats.
  • Deep learning skills: training networks, tensor handling, data splitting and augmentation.
  • Forestry knowledge: domain understanding of tropical forests to interpret model outputs appropriately.

Additional Notes

The internship emphasized mastering TomoSAR formats (covariance matrices) and combining DL models with traditional methods to improve height estimation accuracy.
