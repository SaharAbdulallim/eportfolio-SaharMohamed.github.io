About Me

I'm an Erasmus Mundus scholar in the Copernicus Master in Digital Earth program, currently based in Strasbourg, France. My work sits at the intersection of satellite remote sensing, deep learning, and operational geospatial systems.

My master's thesis at iCube-SERTIT (Strasbourg University) & IRISA/OBELIX (UBS Vannes) focuses on developing automated burnt area detection pipelines for the Copernicus Emergency Management Service (CEMS) using Very High-Resolution SPOT imagery and multi-task semantic segmentation.

Previously I worked on TomoSAR deep learning for canopy height estimation, rice paddy detection in Thailand via Sentinel-2, and contributed a QGIS plugin (VirtuGhan) to the open-source community. My background combines space navigation engineering from Egypt with cutting-edge European AI research.
